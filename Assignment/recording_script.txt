Vuvuzela denoising recording script

1. Load audio
I start by loading the noisy speech signal and the vuvuzela reference 
signal. If the reference has a different sampling rate, I resample 
it so both signals are processed at the same rate.

2. Plot input signals
Next, I plot both signals in the time domain. This shows that the 
interference is a repeating tonal noise.

3. Estimate harmonic structure
I then estimate the vuvuzela fundamental and harmonics from the 
reference PSD. The harmonic peaks show where the buzzing energy is 
concentrated, so these frequencies become the main cancellation 
targets.

4. Compare spectra
I compare the noisy and reference spectra to decide the passband and 
stopband regions. The speech information is mainly in the mid band, 
while the buzz is strongest at the harmonic frequencies.

5. STFT suppression
I use an STFT-based adaptive filter stage to suppress the interference 
in the frequency domain. This stage combines Wiener-like suppression, 
spectral subtraction, and harmonic masking.

to satisfy delay limit I set 1024-sample STFT frame and a
 256-sample hop at 8 kHz. That gives a 128 ms frame and about 64 ms 
 effective frame-center delay, which is below the 0.2 second limit.

6. Notch cascade and cleanup
After that, I apply a cascade of narrow notch filters at the harmonic 
frequencies and nearby drift values. I also apply light bandpass and 
high-pass to keep the speech clear.
